Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from parents, prospective cadets, and community members.

What are the Richardson Police Public Safety Cadets?

The Public Safety Cadets is a youth program sponsored by the Richardson Police Department. It provides young people ages 14–20 with hands-on training in law enforcement procedures, leadership development, and community service. Cadets attend weekly meetings, participate in ride-alongs, and compete in regional competitions.

What are the age requirements to join?

Cadets must be between 14 and 20 years old. You must also be enrolled in school or have completed your GED, maintain a minimum 2.0 GPA, and have no felony convictions or pending criminal charges.

When and where do cadets meet?

Cadets meet every Thursday from 6:00 PM to 9:00 PM at the Richardson Police Department. Optional physical training (PT) is available at 5:45 PM before the meeting and at 9:00 PM after — each session is 15 minutes.

Is there a cost to join?

The program is free to join. Cadets are provided uniforms and training materials. Some optional activities like competitions or special events may have associated costs, but the unit works to minimize out-of-pocket expenses.

Do cadets have law enforcement authority?

No. Cadets are civilian volunteers and have no law enforcement authority whatsoever. This is clearly stated on all identification cards issued to cadets. The program is educational and prepares young people who may be interested in pursuing a career in law enforcement or public safety.

What do cadets learn during training?

Training covers a wide range of law enforcement topics including traffic stops, domestic violence response, active threat scenarios, crime scene investigation, patrol tactics, report writing, radio communications, and more. Training is scenario-based and hands-on whenever possible.

What are ride-alongs?

Ride-alongs allow cadets to accompany patrol officers during their shifts. Cadets observe real police work in action. To participate, cadets must complete the Ride Along Academy certification and submit a Ride-Along Request form approved by their advisor and the patrol supervisor.

What is the probation period?

New cadets enter a 6-month probationary period. During this time, cadets are expected to attend meetings regularly, maintain proper conduct, and demonstrate commitment to the program. After successfully completing probation, cadets become full members.

Can this program help with a career in law enforcement?

Absolutely. Many former cadets have gone on to careers in law enforcement, fire service, EMS, and military. The program provides real-world experience, mentorship from active officers, and leadership skills that are valuable in any career path.
